# ADR-0002: Headline aim is the evolution of questioning/conceptualization

**Status:** Accepted · 2026-07-10

## Context

The original brief framed the headline as *learning intern vs. static co-scientist* (a performance comparison). The background review showed a fair static-co-scientist baseline is expensive and easy to strawman (the real Co-Scientist itself learns by growing the prompt). The owner's actual interest is not a scoreboard but *how learning/questioning evolves*.

## Decision

The headline object of study is **how research questioning and disease conceptualization evolve** — characterized across a human reference panel (college intern → master's → medical → resident) and an AI intern across cycles. Human and AI are complementary windows on one developmental process, not competitors.

## Consequences

- Learning-vs-static is **demoted** to a simple Loop-C on/off ablation, not the headline.
- The question trajectory + concept-model snapshots become the primary instruments.
- Measurement is descriptive/developmental (trajectory-against-waypoints), honest at small N; not a performance benchmark.
- Alternative rejected: build a publication-grade co-scientist baseline — out of scope for a feasibility pilot and not what the owner is asking.
