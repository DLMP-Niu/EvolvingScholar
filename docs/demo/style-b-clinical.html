A question for a trainee

Can an AI intern climb the same competency ladder a clinician does?

EvolvingScholar is an AI research intern that learns the way a trainee does — guided by a mentor, project by project — and we can measure how far up the ladder it climbs.

Where this started
"Review five years of literature on gene A and disease B, and study the largest US patient cohort. How far toward true expertise does that take you?"

— the question posed to a real research intern, on day one

EvolvingScholar lets us run that project quickly across many classic clinical-genetics diseases — and read the growth as a trajectory, not a claim of human-equivalent expertise.

EPA — Entrustable Professional Activity: a unit of clinical work a trainee can be trusted to perform. It is how real competence is credentialed in medicine.

And it noticed something

An intern that catches a coding error — and remembers it.

41 patients

coded under legacy ICD-9 277.3x instead of the current E85.x — so a naïve amyloidosis filter would have silently missed them. The scholar flagged it, corrected the cohort, and wrote the lesson back as a lasting capability.
